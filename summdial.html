SummDial

A SIGDial 2021 Special Session on Automatic Minuting and Multi-party Meeting/Dialogue Summarization

Ever since most of our interactions went virtual, the need for automatic support for smooth running (continuous streaming) of online events and meetings became more intense. Summarizing meeting contents is one of them. Meeting minutes keep a record of what was discussed at a meeting. It is usually a rather unstructured written document (beyond some possible shallow hierarchical structure) used to inform participants and non-participants of what happened during the meeting. Automatic Minuting tools would be an useful addition to better comprehend the meeting contents quickly. With the overload of online meetings, imagine how convenient it would be to just hover over past calendar invites and get concise summaries of the meeting proceedings? How about minuting a multimodal multi-party meeting? There are possibly no agreed-upon guidelines to take minutes and people adopt different styles to record minutes of the meeting. The minutes also depend on the category of the meeting, the intended audience, and the goal or objective of the meeting. Dialogue summarization would rank close to this task. We want to discuss all of these challenges in our SummDial special session at SIGDial 2021. We are also launching the first shared task on Automatic Minuting (AutoMin) at Interspeech 2021. Our goal for this SIGDial special session would be to stimulate intense discussions around this topic and also to set the tone for further interest, research and collaboration in both Speech and Natural Language Processing communities. SummDial @ SIGDial 2021 will be an all-virtual event.
