Shared Task Overview

Summarizing meetings in the form of structured minutes from speech can potentially save up to 80% of time. The First Shared Task on Automatic Minuting is aimed towards creating minutes from multi-party meetings. Minuting is closely related to summarization, however, both are not exactly the same task. Automatic Minuting is challenging in the sense that there is no universal framework for creating minutes and it varies across different types of meetings, subjects, and objective. Our shared task would consist of one main task and two supporting tasks for two different kinds of meeting scenarios (technical meetings and parliamentary proceedings). The technical meetings would consist of data in English and also in Czech. A closely related shared task on Speech Summarization was carried out in 2006 with a focus on English. In the light of the increased dominance of deep learning and efficient language models in Speech and Natural Language Processing it is an exciting time to re-launching such a shared task effort. The objective is to rejuvenate the community interest to attempt this interesting problem and unfold challenges towards creating a unified framework for automatic minuting of meetings.
